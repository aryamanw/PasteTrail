Install in seconds

Distributed as a Homebrew tap. Builds from source on your machine — no notarization, no Apple account needed.

1

Add the tap and install:

$ brew tap aryamanw/pastetrail
$ brew install pastetrail
2

Move the app to Applications:

$ cp -r $(brew --prefix)/opt/pastetrail/PasteTrail.app /Applications/
3

Launch Paste Trail from Applications, then grant Accessibility access when prompted:

System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility

Frequently asked questions

Which Mac models does this work on?

Any Mac running macOS 13 Ventura or later. That's most Macs from 2017 onward.

Does it send my data anywhere?

No. Zero network calls. Your clipboard stays on your machine. Verified with Little Snitch — you can verify it yourself.

Why only 20 clips?

20 covers the vast majority of real-world use — going back further than that is surprisingly rare. The rolling window keeps things snappy and the SQLite database tiny. It's free and open source, so if you want more, you can change the cap yourself.

Why does it need Accessibility access?

Accessibility is the only way for a background app to synthesise ⌘V into whichever app you're working in. Without it, Paste Trail can show your clips but can't paste them. It uses no other Accessibility API.

Will it capture my passwords?

No. Paste Trail automatically ignores 1Password, Bitwarden, and Keychain. Those never make it into your history.

Is it really free?

Completely. No paid tier, no license key, no telemetry. It's MIT-licensed open source — read every line if you like.
